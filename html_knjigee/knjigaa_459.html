Carol Denise Mitchell

On September 1, 2022, Ruthless Pamela Jean became author Carol Denise Mitchell's 2nd Readers Favorite Book Award-winning novel. The author is thrilled! In this amazing youth adventure, Pamela Jean Metcalf’s father left his daughter at birth, not believing a baby so light-skinned could be his. In Carol Denise Mitchell’s ground-breaking book, Ruthless Pamela Jean, the author leads readers on a real-life roller coaster ride, where they’re dropped off into the life of one “only” child, who aims to put an end to racial child bullying. There is nothing conventional about Ruthless Pamela Jean, who is heartbroken when she sees her beloved mother Betty, her best friend, praying at the foot of her bed that the bullying against her light-skinned daughter will end. Upon hearing her mother’s prayers, little Pamela Jean Metcalf leaves her broken mother out of her daily issues of abuse as she sets out to change the formidable child-bullying game!

The author dares to make Iceberg Slim, the 60s most notorious "Pimp,” Pamela Jean’s go-to mentor! Mitchell says, “Why not?" After reading "PIMP" by Iceberg Slim, Pamela embraces Slim’s controlling metaphors to handle bullies in a dark tunnel that tried to kill her. There, Pamela builds her strength. She takes the bullies by the horn(s) while she fights back. One day of strength gives Pamela Jean Metcalf a lifetime of hope. Readers will engage with this “think-out-the-box” book like no other! Pamela’s initial survival comes from state psychiatrist Nancy Parker, who becomes PJ’s lifelong mentor. The doctor convinces Pamela to confront the abuse without violence by using her muses’ metaphors to build character and become a winner. What emerges is a woman in control of a man’s game. Survival means Pamela would enter the man’s game armed with an Ivy League education to create a very successful male escort startup that turns into pure gold. Pamela experiences a meteoric halt in the nation’s abuse as she sets out to save the world. Notwithstanding, as Pamela adheres to the doctor’s counseling, she is empowered by tragedy; she’s a warrior for the abused. From a broken child into one of the nation’s most powerful business women and a spokesperson against child bullying, Pamela Jean Metcalf emerges nicely as a savvy businesswoman who teaches the bullied how to take control and win!

If you’re looking for a fast-paced and action-packed thrill of a read that will stay with you long after completing the last page, then you need look no further – Ruthless Pamela Jean is the book you must add to your reading list immediately! Difficult to read in places but extremely eye-opening, and with a morally grey protagonist at its heart (ruthless she most certainly is!), this is a novel that will have you gripped from beginning to end.

Ultimately, Ruthless Pamela Jean is an examination of what it means to be human, how to succeed against all odds and break free from the confines of society. Placing emphasis on issues such as race and bullying, Carol Denise Mitchell is not afraid to confront heavy – and at times rather difficult to read – topics that really delve deep into the raw human experience. While I was certainly left with plenty to think about, I really appreciated how Mitchell didn’t let such topics and discussions overtake the novel or become heavy-handed in any way. The plot itself, which sees Pamela Jean grow from a young girl into a strong businesswoman with plenty of obstacles to overcome along the way, is thrilling in its own right, but the ongoing inclusion of the heavier themes really helped to enhance my reading experience and help me to connect with our protagonist on a deeper level. Mitchell’s handing of this is to be hugely commended, and her writing throughout was a joy to read.

I don’t want to share too much about the plot here because it sure is a rollercoaster of a ride that I implore you to experience for yourself, but I will say that regardless of your preferred genre you’re sure to find something in here to love. Ruthless Pamela Jean is packed full to the brim with drama, romance and plenty of high-stakes action, this is a book you’ll want to devour in one sitting. Highly recommend!

This gripping story about an abused girl, who uses the unlikely prototype of a pimp as a guide to a better life, acts as a sort of metaphor of social realism regarding the rise of the African Americans to greater prominence and wealth in modern society as a whole. The narrative, which is closely written, and uses a realistic version of African American speech soon draws us into the central dilemma of a child, who due to her light skin, is rejected by the other children in her school, this then leads to her being ostracized, bullied and at one point even her life is threatened.

When she retaliates, in order to save herself, the system unfairly punishes her. She is sentenced to juvenile detention. In many cases that would be the end of the story, with a predicted descent into a spiral of ever more crime and degradation. But two things save her from this likely future. Her own qualities of intelligence and determination and the interest taken in her by a lady child psychologist who encourages her in her studies even to the extent of standing by her in the years that follow.

Female sexual frustration is not something that often appears in the media which tends to center upon ladies complaining about the unwelcome attentions of men, but this book tends to show that many older women are deprived of fulfilment of their intimate needs by social niceties and prudery. Pamela is acutely aware of this problem and does something to provide relief for these women – at a price.

The ingenious way in which Pamela sets up something that purports to be a charity for the aid of children bullied in the school yard as a front for her male prostitution business is well described, as are the problems she runs into in running such a business.

This is a very satisfying read which I highly recommend.

This is an interesting and entertaining read that exposes these issues in a sensitive way while showing how one damaged woman dealt with them and succeeded.

Ruthless Pamela Jean by Carol Denise Mitchell is one of the best stories that you will ever come across. This urban fiction is unique and exceptionally well written. It is fast paced and intriguing with an incredible storyline which would undoubtedly keep you hooked.

'When the going gets tough, the tough get going', Pamela Jean is the epitome of perseverance, facing various hurdles in life with her unwavering resolution.

"All my life I have had to fight for everything. One time I was nearly burnt in a tunnel because of this face". Her light skin, big pink lips and flaming red hair brought her misery from a young age as she was continuously abused and bullied for her peculiarity among the black community. However her resilience helps her to achieve her goals.

It is quite unusual how an eight-year-old finds solace and confidence through a book written by a pimp called "Iceberg Slim" whom she considers a role model. She herself wishes to start an escort service and ultimately does so while she stands up against bullying. That itself portrays that she is a remarkable character.

Mitchell also sheds light on how love can change a person, through the roles of Nancy Parker and Pamela's mom Betty Justine Metcalf. Her mom raises PJ on her own after the dad leaves as soon as the baby was born claiming the baby to be someone else's. Betty does her best to give a good life to her beloved daughter who is ostracized by society.

After she tells her mom about the name calling and bullying her mom becomes deeply saddened and later seeing her mom pray and cry, young Pamela decides to not bring that up again and to fight her own demons. This causes her to internalize her pain which ultimately leads to her outburst at the tunnel.

However, the incident makes it possible for PJ to meet the wonderful and kindhearted psychiatrist Nancy, who treats PJ not as a mere patient but embraces her broken self with much love and fully supports her education and future aspirations.

Mitchell walks us through the life of Pamela through her ups and downs and unexpected events in a truly cinematic manner.

Lastly, I wish to mention a part which struck a chord, "PJ was right to assume that no matter what path she took in life, she would be judged, even in a soup kitchen, where she wanted to help" this sentence resonates how judgemental people will remain to be.

This book is amazing. I absolutely loved it. It's intense and engaging. I'm sure you would be itching to find out what happens next as you keep reading.
I highly recommend this masterpiece!

This book is not an easy read. That doesn't mean it's not a good read.
The way people turn out is a combination of who we are inside and how others see (and treat) us. Nature Vs Nurture. Ruthless Pamela Jean takes this concept and runs with it like the wind.
We're introduced to the heroine as an 8-year-old. A girl who is ostracised because she has boyish looks and pale skin despite being obviously black. She attends a school where her peers are African Americans and is bullied both physically and mentally.
Pamela does not react the way many children would. She internalises her hurt and determines to be a stronger person because of it. Unfortunately, she begins to do so in a negative way.
Without giving the story away, I can reveal that we follow her life well into adulthood as she builds a huge business empire on questionable grounds.
Will she find happiness in the end? You'll have to read and see.
My biggest problem with this book was understanding the language used. I'm an Australian, and I often had to re-read a passage to understand what was actually being said. The dialogue and narration rely heavily on urban Afro-American speech. That's my problem, however, and I refuse to let it taint my assessment.
All in all, I think fans of this genre will definitely enjoy Ruthless Pamela Jean.

Ruthless Pamela Jean

This unique and fast moving thriller promises to be everything Pamela Jean is in the books' title - utterly ruthless and will stop at nothing to get what she wants. She is a woman on a mission. What drives this woman is the deep emotional scars she has due to the intollerable and gut-wrenching bullying she endured as a child because of her unusual looks. One day, in order to defend herself, she finally takes matters into her own hands. This turnaround finally gives her the power and confidence she needs to take on the world.

Over the next 20 years and inspired and driven by her love of one particular story (that of the pimp Iceberg Slim), she conceives a grand plan to create a high end male escort agency for rich, ageing women. Given that this is an 'illegal' venture she hides the business behind a charity to raise funds for child hood bullying. She believes that every customer will donate towards the cause thus raising millions.

The story twists and turns as she develops her business, careering head on into shady and unstrustworthy characters such as Kendra, Patrice and Raymond - not to mention Marla who is equally determined and becomes one of the most important influences in her life.

It is a racy and pacy thriller which will keep you guessing how Ruthless Pamela gets herself out of the challenges that come her way - and all to get what she really wants. This is a well written and unique thriller and will keep you interested right up to the end.

Carol Mitchell’s book “Ruthless Pamela Jean”, is gritty and unsettling.

The reader is pulled through the plight of Pamela Jean which is entrenched in racial disparity.
The reader is inside the shadowy mind of a woman, who’s life is drenched in brutality and misfortune.

Pamala Jean (PJ) is a biracial young women who’s life has been unbearable due to social disadvantage, and ongoing ridicule due to the shade of her skin. Her mother Betty tries to protect her daughter while she can, but is limited in means and empowerment.

Years of being ostracised from PJ’s peers and family, heavily contributes to her violent and almost psychopathic behaviour when encountering other people, especially those who have done her wrong.

Confused, angry, and bitter, Pamala Jean wants to live out a normal life.
But years of abuse has made her wary of humankind and is vengeful.

Her muse is a fictitious character called “Pimp”, from a book she finds on the bus called "Iceberg Slim". In her delusions, PJ fashions her personality in line with the Pimp's indifference to other people.

Pamala Jean fantasies that retribution by ways of “pimping” out her enemies - is the road to her mental and emotional salvation - a sort of perverse power play.

Pamala Jean's life is a roller coster of well earned wins and devastating losses.

Ms Michell has given insight into the struggles and discrimination of being biracial, and yet champions the many obstacles which are overcome.

Ruthless Pamela Jean is an Urban realism book. This is not a fairy tale but a story of the battles of good and bad within an American city - the language and style reflects this genre.
A must read for anyone wanting a realistic look into the the challenging, often ugly lives of people living on the fringes of our society.
